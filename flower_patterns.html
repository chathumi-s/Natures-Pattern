Patterns in

FLOWERS

Flowers are often the most striking parts of plants, featuring petals arranged in beautiful patterns that attract pollinators and help with reproduction. The way petals are arranged, their symmetry, and the color patterns are all designed to draw attention while also providing protection.

Types of Patterns in Flowers

Radial Symmetry

Seen in flowers like daisies and lilies

Purpose - Radial symmetry lets pollinators approach flowers from any angle. Each petal is evenly spaced around a central point, which helps guide insects to the nectar and pollen.

Bilateral Symmetry

Found in orchids and snapdragons

Purpose - This pattern looks like a mirror image, with the flower split down the middle. This design directs pollinators along a specific path, which increases the chances of successful pollination.

Functions of Flower Patterns

Pollinator Attraction

Colorful and symmetrical petal arrangements attract insects, birds, and bats, which helps in cross-pollination.

Protection

Some flower patterns feature hidden markings called nectar guides. These markings help guide pollinators to the nectar while also protecting the plant's reproductive parts.
