MOUNTAINS

Mountains are shaped by tectonic shifts, volcanoes, and erosion, creating layers, folds, and unique formations. These features reveal Earthâ€™s geological history and support many life forms adapted to the tough terrain.

Types of Patterns in Mountains

Layered patterns

Each layer represents different sediment deposits formed over millions of years. These layers tell a story of ancient rivers, seas, and volcanic eruptions.

Folded patterns

Folded mountains, like the Himalayas, form when tectonic plates collide, pushing up the land into wavelike crests and valleys. This creates dramatic and stunning landscapes.

Jagged peaks and valleys

Weathering and erosion sculpt sharp peaks and deep valleys, giving mountains their rugged beauty

How Patterns Help?

Ecosystem Diversity

Different heights and slopes on a mountain create varied climates, allowing many types of plants and animals to thrive in one place.

Water Storage and Release

Mountains capture moisture, forming glaciers and streams that slowly release water to nearby valleys. This water is vital for local ecosystems and nearby communities.

Soil Formation

As mountain rocks erode, they create mineral-rich soil that supports plant growth. This soil benefits both mountain species and farming in lower areas.
